Chapter One
The Fixed
Mindset
When intelligence feels like a ceiling.
✕
Avoids Challenges
If it might expose weakness, it's not worth trying. Safety comes before growth.
✕
Blames the Tool, Not the Prompt
When the model fails, it's the model's fault. The prompt was fine. It had to be.
✕
Treats Others as Threats
Someone else's success is a reminder of your own inadequacy. Competition, not collaboration.
✕
Needs to Look Smart Right Now
Being seen as capable matters more than actually becoming capable. Proving over improving.
Chapter Two
The Growth
Mindset
When every failure is a data point.
✓
Runs Toward Challenges
Hard problems are the point. Struggle means you're operating at the edge of your ability — exactly where growth happens.
✓
Reads Bad Output as Data
The model hallucinated? Good — now you know what the prompt was missing. Every failure is a signal, not a verdict.
✓
Sees Peers as Allies
Other people's breakthroughs are blueprints, not threats. Borrow their thinking and go further than either of you could alone.
✓
Becomes Rather Than Proves
Sacrifices the comfort of today's identity to build a better one. The direction matters more than the current position.
